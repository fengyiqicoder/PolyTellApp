1. Polymarket

Liquidity Metrics

Polymarket's liquidity is heavily concentrated, with a noticeable divide between thriving markets and those that remain inactive. For instance, long-term markets average around $450,000 in liquidity, while ultra-short-term markets operate with a much smaller average of $10,000. This highlights the platform's selective liquidity dynamics.

To better understand market behavior, Polymarket employs advanced metrics beyond basic spreads. The Flow-Impact Lambda measures how much the mid-price shifts (in basis points) per unit of signed taker flow — lower values here indicate markets that recover quickly after large trades. Another key metric, the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI), assesses liquidity provider concentration. A lower HHI score reflects healthier competition among makers. A notable example occurred on February 17, 2026, when a $70,000 liquidity reward was injected into the "Will Jesus Christ return before 2027?" market. This caused the HHI to drop significantly from 0.164 to 0.041, as the number of active makers jumped from 302 to 639.

"Polymarket is evolving from a 'predict anything' utopia into an extremely specialized financial tool. Value is only discovered where liquidity is abundant; where liquidity dries up, there are only traps." — Frank, Author, PANews

These metrics reflect the platform's focus on leveraging sophisticated tools to enhance its liquidity ecosystem.

AI-Driven Features

Polymarket integrates historical L2 data and API access, enabling traders to create AI agents with frameworks like OpenClaw. These agents automate liquidity strategies with predefined constraints. For example, an agent might hold off on trades if the spread exceeds 60 basis points, allowing traders to simulate order execution before committing capital. Tools like Polyseer further enhance decision-making by analyzing resolution criteria and key data sources, such as FRED indicators and SEC filings. This helps identify mispriced markets where traders may be reacting to headlines rather than underlying probabilities.

In 2025, Polymarket introduced a non-linear taker fee model for crypto markets. This fee structure, based on probabilities, redistributes costs through maker rebates. The result? Average bid-ask spreads in 15-minute crypto markets narrowed from 4.5% in 2023 to 1.2% by 2025. Additionally, bot-driven wash trading dropped from 25% to 5% by May 2025. During the week of February 2–8, 2026, these markets processed 15.3 million on-chain fills worth $153 million, with Bitcoin dominating 19 of the top 20 most profitable wallets.

User Accessibility

Polymarket combines its technical sophistication with a user-friendly experience, making it accessible to a wide range of traders. The platform supports various funding methods, including cryptocurrency, credit/debit cards, and bank transfers. Its tiered interface caters to both beginners and advanced users, offering "simple" and "advanced" modes. Even users who don't actively trade can monitor market sentiment for free, turning Polymarket into a "real-time probabilistic newsfeed" that attracts organic traffic and boosts liquidity.

After acquiring QCEX, a CFTC-licensed exchange, Polymarket launched a regulated U.S. mobile app in December 2025, aiming to attract mainstream retail users. By early 2026, the platform had reached 1.6 million unique users, with 61.48% of them holding positions between $0 and $50. Interestingly, 14.8% of wallets show net realized gains — nearly five times higher than the 3% seen in traditional sports betting.

FAQs

Why is Polymarket liquidity so concentrated in a small number of markets?

Polymarket's liquidity tends to cluster around a handful of markets, driven by user activity and the nature of market dynamics. High-profile events, such as political outcomes, draw the most attention and, consequently, the bulk of liquidity. The distribution of profits reflects this imbalance, with a small group of traders capturing the lion's share of earnings. Additionally, the presence of large traders and the appeal of short-term, high-volatility markets contribute to this concentration, steering focus toward prominent events rather than a more diverse range of markets.

How do Flow-Impact Lambda and HHI measure liquidity quality?

Flow-Impact Lambda measures the quality of liquidity by analyzing how trading activity influences market prices. It reflects how responsive and efficient liquidity is within a market. Meanwhile, the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) evaluates the concentration of liquidity. Lower HHI values suggest that liquidity is spread more evenly across various markets. When combined, these two metrics offer a detailed view of how liquidity performs and behaves.
