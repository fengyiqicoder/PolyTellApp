FAQs

How do prediction market odds turn into real probabilities?

Prediction markets turn market prices into percentage-based probabilities, offering a simple way to gauge the likelihood of an event. For instance, if a contract is trading at $0.65, it suggests a 65% chance that the event will happen. These prices represent the collective judgment of traders, pooling together a variety of information to create a probabilistic forecast. This approach relies on crowd-sourced data and market consensus to estimate outcomes effectively.

What makes a market "trustworthy" (volume, liquidity, whales)?

A reliable prediction market thrives on high trading volume and strong liquidity, which are essential for active participation and accurate price setting. Additionally, the involvement of "whales" - traders with substantial investments - can help stabilize the market by contributing informed perspectives and significant stakes. Together, these elements foster an environment where predictions are dependable and decisions are well-informed.
